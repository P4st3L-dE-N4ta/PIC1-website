Doing it ourselves!

May 11th, 2025 • 1min and 59s read time

Summary

Hello again! This week proved itself to be slightly bittersweet for us. On the one hand, we still haven’t received the materials we ordered even though we ordered them quite a while ago, which is setting us a tiny bit back every single day, but we still managed to progress on the hardware department using some electronics material owned by Afonso. Even though we’re not working under ideal conditions, we still try to find ways to work around the unfortunate circumstances.

Hardware and software progress

While working on the prototype during the week, we started by writing an Arduino program capable of calculating the BER of a snippet from a random binary transmission. This algorithm may also help us to later be able to distinguish between each type of error (either random errors or, for instance, burst errors, etc.), and to hopefully be able to apply different types of ECC’s to each type of error.

However, there was also quite a big leap on the hardware department. Using an LM741CN OpAmp, we managed to improve the IV receptor, going from having a BER value of 0 at a distance of 5 cm to having that same value at 70 cm, which is a 1400% increase in efficacy! And even with these 70 cm we still have a very low SNR value, which makes us believe that we might be able to amplify the signal even more without sacrificing its quality. The future seems bright!

Weekly meeting with our coordinators

Lastly, on Friday we had the usual meeting with our project coordinators. Even though this is merely a routine project check-up, we did discuss some important matters about the ElectroDay presentation and some other matters.

Since the presentation will in reality be a sampling of the prototype in an already small (and quite conditioned) environment, it means that we are allowed to cut some corners. And well, given that the implementation of the interleaving algorithm is proving itself to be more complicated than we originally anticipated, our counselling professors advised us “not to get lost in things that aren’t totally necessary”, which means that we are contemplating not doing the interleaving part.

About the presentation itself, we are planning on demoing the prototype in a small see-through water tank and having different types of messages (audio, video, pictures, etc.) being transmitted in a way that can make it possible for the attendees to hear and/or see the process in real time, either through a speaker or a screen.

Conclusion

Another week goes by and we still haven’t received the materials we ordered. If another week passes, we might start to get a bit preoccupied. But for now, we remain optimistic and try to progress through our own means. So, see ya next week!
